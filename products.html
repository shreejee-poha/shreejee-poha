Poha

Raw poha, also known as flattened rice, is a popular and light Indian staple made from parboiled rice. It is a versatile ingredient used to create both savory and sweet dishes, such as the famous "Poha," making it a quick and delicious choice for breakfast or snacks.




Paddy

Paddy, the unhusked rice grain, is a vital staple crop cultivated in many parts of the world. It serves as the primary source of rice production, playing a significant role in global food security and traditional cuisines. Paddy undergoes various milling processes to yield white or brown rice, which is a dietary staple for billions of people.




Poha Churi

Poha churi is often used as cattle feed, providing nutrition to animals. It is created from the leftover or crunched parts of poha, reducing food waste and serving as a sustainable option for livestock.




Bhusa/Konda

Bhusa/Konda is a granulated organic byproduct of poha used primarily as cattle feed, rich in fiber and nutrients. Additionally, it serves as a valuable source of fuel for rural households and is characterized by its light brown color.
